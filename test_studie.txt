Study: Nutritional Benefits of Flaxseed and Chia Seeds for Cardiovascular Health
Published in: Journal of Nutrition Research, 2024
Pages: 145-158

Abstract:
This meta-analysis examined the effects of flaxseed and chia seeds on cardiovascular health markers.

Key Findings:

FLAXSEED (Leinsamen):
Flaxseed is one of the richest plant sources of alpha-linolenic acid (ALA), a type of omega-3 fatty acid.
Regular consumption of 2 tablespoons (20g) ground flaxseed daily was associated with:
- 15% reduction in LDL cholesterol (p<0.01)
- Significant improvement in blood pressure regulation
- Enhanced gut microbiome diversity due to high fiber content

Nutritional profile per 100g:
- Protein: 18.3g
- Carbohydrates: 28.9g
- Fat: 42.2g (of which 22.8g Omega-3 ALA)
- Fiber: 27.3g

Health claims supported by strong evidence (RCT/Meta-analysis):
1. Flaxseed consumption supports heart health through omega-3 fatty acids
2. High fiber content promotes gut health and digestive regularity
3. Lignans in flaxseed may have anti-inflammatory properties

CHIA SEEDS (Chiasamen):
Chia seeds are nutrient-dense seeds from Salvia hispanica, native to Central America.
Studies show regular consumption improves:
- Blood sugar control in type 2 diabetes patients
- Sustained energy release due to slow carbohydrate absorption
- Bone health through high calcium content

Nutritional profile per 100g:
- Protein: 16.5g
- Carbohydrates: 42.1g
- Fat: 30.7g (of which 17.8g Omega-3 ALA)
- Fiber: 34.4g
- Calcium: 631mg

Health claims supported by moderate evidence (Cohort studies):
1. Chia seeds support blood sugar balance through fiber and slow-release carbs
2. High omega-3 content contributes to brain health
3. Excellent source of plant-based calcium for bone health

WALNUTS (Walnüsse):
Walnuts contain the highest amount of omega-3 fatty acids among tree nuts.
A handful (30g) daily was associated with improved cognitive function in older adults.

Nutritional profile per 100g:
- Protein: 15.2g
- Carbohydrates: 13.7g
- Fat: 65.2g (of which 9.1g Omega-3 ALA)
- Fiber: 6.7g
- Vitamin E: 0.7mg
- Magnesium: 158mg

Health claims supported by strong evidence:
1. Walnuts support brain health and cognitive function
2. Regular consumption reduces inflammation markers
3. Associated with reduced cardiovascular disease risk

Conclusion:
These plant-based foods represent excellent additions to a heart-healthy diet.
The combination of omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and antioxidants provides synergistic benefits for cardiovascular and metabolic health.
